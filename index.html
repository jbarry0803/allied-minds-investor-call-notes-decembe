4. Resolutions for the 23 December General Meeting

Resolution 1 — Share buyback from Classic Partners

  • Repurchase of 2.1 million shares for £1,000 in total (stated to be below nominal value).
  • Management description: requested by Classic Partners for administrative reasons (removing the holding from their register by year-end).
  • Impact: reduces ALM's share count by approximately 1%.
  • The buyback is limited to these specific shares. Management cited US Investment Company Act 1940 considerations as the reason for not opening it more broadly to retail holders.
  • Estimated legal and administrative cost: ~£15,000.

Resolution 2 — Profit share scheme for directors

  • Beneficiaries: directors (Sam and Mark); possible extension to consultants.
  • Hurdle: $31.6m of distributions to shareholders must be paid before any payout under the scheme.
  • $31.6m is described by management as the value point at which the company delisted.
  • Above the hurdle: 10% of any excess accrues to the scheme.
  • The 10% rate applies until 31 December 2027, then steps down to 8% in 2028, then declines by 1% per year thereafter.
  • The rate does not step up for an earlier exit.
  • Sam's salary is being reduced from £200,000 to a non-executive director fee equivalent of $75,000 (the same as Mark) at year-end.

5. Questions and Answers

Q. Probability of a portfolio company sale within six months?
Federated: management considers it premature to expect a sale before regulatory clarity on high-power CBRS. OSK: still being explored; proceeds to ALM, if any, expected to be very small.
Q. What is ALM's holding in Federated, and how does the waterfall work?
Stated holding: 32% of common shares undiluted; 24% fully diluted. Capital stack: approximately $200m of preferred shares (Series D carries a 6% increasing dividend; current liquidation preference cited as ~$215m). Cerberus warrants of 2.6% at each of $350m, $400m and $450m valuations. Management noted the fully-diluted 24% figure becomes the relevant one above approximately $500m. Base case return to ALM cited as ~$35m. Management stated that 60–70% of incremental value above $200m accrues to ALM. Management's recommendation: model on the diluted basis.
Q. Can ALM influence the outcome at Federated?
ALM holds 3 of 8 board seats and chairs the Audit & Finance Committee. Any transaction requires a super-majority, which gives ALM a blocking position.
Q. Is further dilution anticipated at Federated?
Management does not anticipate further dilution unless circumstances change materially. Cerberus's full-ratchet protection means a raise below approximately $302m valuation would not be considered.
Q. Why has revenue grown only 36% if market share has gone from 50% to 77%?
Management cited the limited size of the CBRS market itself (effective duopoly with Google, who is exiting marketing of the product). Future growth is expected to come from deeper deployment with key customers (Verizon was cited) and from pricing. Mark stated that the board has instructed management to raise average selling prices given the market position.
Q. How does management value Federated?
No specific valuation was provided. Management stated they want the business assessed as a recurring-revenue software business rather than a telco, citing 60%+ gross margins and 30–40% growth. Strategic optionality cited: extension to other spectrum bands (e.g. AFC) and international application (UK and Saudi Arabia were mentioned as exploring shared spectrum).
Q. What is ALM's holding company cash runway?
Approximately two years. The cost base has been reduced from ~$5m/year (2022) to a stated ~$400,000/year from 2026.
Q. Do directors receive any compensation from Federated?
No. The ALM incentive scheme is paid in cash and only after the $31.6m distribution hurdle is cleared. Management noted that treatment in the event of a Federated flotation would need to be revisited, but stated that flotation is not the expected exit path.
Q. Status of Cerberus (activist shareholder)?
Management described the relationship as currently aligned, with both parties working toward a similar exit timeframe. The relationship was previously strained around the Bridge Bank default risk earlier in the year.
Q. EGM notice not received / website not loading?
Investors were directed to MUFG (the registrar) or to their broker for documentation. The website issue was acknowledged and was to be checked.

6. Stated Exit Plan

  • Management's stated goal is an exit on Federated in 2027.
  • The next 12–18 months are described as the period in which the SaaS valuation thesis must be demonstrated.
  • OSK is not expected to contribute meaningful proceeds.
  • Two years of holding company runway is cited as cover for this timeline.
  • Mark noted that placing a valuation on Federated today is difficult; that exercise is what 2026 is intended to enable.

7. Management's Closing Ask

Management asked shareholders to vote in favour of both resolutions at the 23 December General Meeting.
